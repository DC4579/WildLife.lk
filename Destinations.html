Yala National Park is located 260km South East of Colombo. It is the most visited and second largest national park in Sri Lanka, bordering the Indian Ocean. The park is known for its variety of wildlife, especially leopards and elephants1.
Yala National Park is located 260km southeast of Colombo and covers an area of 378 square miles1. It is known for its high leopard density, which is higher than anywhere else on the planet.
The park is also home to elephants and deer. The best time to visit is from June to September, and it is open from 6am to 6pm.

It is the largest and one of the oldest national parks in Sri Lanka. The park is known for its unique feature of “Willus” (natural lakes) – natural, sand-rimmed water basins or depressions that fill with rainwater2. The park is home to a variety of wildlife, including the endemic purple-faced langur.
