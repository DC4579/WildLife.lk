Animals in Srilanka

indigenous animals

Sri Lankan Leopard (Panthera pardus kotiya)

leopard is a leopard subspecies found only in Sri Lanka. Since the Sri Lankan leopard faces no competition in its habitat and is the apex predator therein, it grows quite large.


Males of this subspecies often weigh as much as 100 kg. You can spot them in national parks such as Yala National Park andWilpattu National Park.

Sri Lankan Elephant:

The Sri Lankan elephant is the largest subspecies of the Asian elephant and is native to Sri Lanka. These elephants have a restricted habitat confined to the dry zone in Sri Lanka’s north, east, and southeast.


They are threatened by human-animal conflicts and habitat destruction. You can spot these majestic creatures in national parks such as Yala National Park andUdawalawe National Park.

Toque Macaque:

The toque macaque is a primate endemic to Sri Lanka. They live in groups of 8 to 40 members and have a broad diet consisting of flowers, fruits, grains, etc. These monkeys have a highly restricted habitat limited to the island of Sri Lanka and are threatened by habitat destruction, hunting, and capture for the pet trade


You can spot them in national parks such a Yala National Park andWilpattu National Park.

Sri Lankan Axis Deer

The Sri Lankan axis deer is a subspecies of the axis deer found only in Sri Lanka. They are known for their distinctive spotted coat and are threatened by habitat loss and hunting


You can spot them in national parks such a Yala National Park andWilpattu National Park.

The Sri Lanka blue magpie

bird species that is endemic to Sri Lanka. It is a member of the family Corvidae and is known for its bright blue plumage, reddish-brown head, neck, and wing, and a long graduated blue tail with a white tip1. The Sri Lanka blue magpie is adapted to hunting in the dense canopy, where it is highly active and nimble


It measures 42-47 cm in length, which is larger than a mynah but smaller than a crow The Sri Lanka blue magpie is classified as vulnerable to extinction due to the fragmentation and destruction of its habitat of dense primary forest in the wet zone of southern Sri Lanka.


You can spot them in Sinharaja Rain Forest,Kitulgala Forest Reserve,The Peak Wilderness Reserveand Hakgala Botanical Gardens
